  • Educational progress

  • Non-cash

1) Verified Yes / No Decision Questions

XPGuess uses simple Yes / No questions to train real-world decision intelligence. Questions are tied to official outcomes — not rumors — and reflect how professional sports decisions are made under pressure.

  • Based on real football decision points

  • XP is awarded for disciplined participation and completion

  • XP represents verified momentum and discipline, not money

Next-Generation Sports Governance: Beyond the Transfer Market

Why the Next Great Athlete Is Not a “Risk” — but a Portfolio

For decades, the path from youth sports to the professional level has been a black box. Agents, scouts, and clubs take massive risks on young talent because they lack a transparent ledger of that athlete’s progress, decision-making ability, and commercial viability.

Across Europe, the legal foundations of FIFA’s transfer system are being challenged. Courts are increasingly recognizing that athletes are workers with rights — not commodities.

After more than 20 years as a collegiate coach and Olympic-level recruiter, I learned that elite performance is driven not just by physical ability, but by decision intelligence and commercial viability. Until now, there was no way to measure that.

1. The Busker Model: Influence Is Capital

In entertainment, organizations like SAG were created to protect young actors from exploitation. Today’s young “actors” are athletes on platforms like TikTok and Instagram.

The problem: Five million video views do not help an athlete earn a scholarship or attract professional attention for the right reasons.

The XPGuess solution: That influence can be imported into the XPGuess ecosystem and converted into XP. This transforms short-lived fame into a verifiable commercial résumé.

2. Manufacturing & Copyright: Building a Home Base

The era of waiting for a Nike contract is over. Inside XPGuess, athletes use XP to manufacture and mint digital items.

  • Ownership: If an athlete mints an item, they own the copyright.
  • Revenue logic: After distribution thresholds, creators can earn from purchases.

This is not a game — it is an intellectual property incubator.

3. Decision Governance: The Yes / No Model

Real sports outcomes are decided in milliseconds — a Yes or a No. My 1997 development model (published in Gymnastics Technique) demonstrated that cognitive load and emotional control drive elite performance.

  • XPGuess trains this pathway through binary decision scenarios
