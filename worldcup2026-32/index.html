📐 The Framework

Step 1: Measure the Gate (SPI)
Did expected teams advance?
First we check if Matchday 3 preserved the competitive ordering from earlier rounds. If the "right" teams went through, the gate held.

Result: 83.3% — in 20 of 24 fixtures, the gate held.
Step 2: Classify the Gate (GEI)
One metric, three thresholds.
GEI = Top Team's Goal Share % (% of all group goals scored by the #1 finisher).

Dominant >60% — Winner overpowered the group (Germany 67%, 10 GF). They created the lopsidedness.

Suppressed 45-60% — Winner was modest in a flat group (Mexico 50%, 6 GF; USA 57%, 8 GF). They inherited the lopsidedness from weak opposition.

Open <45% + SPI held — Balanced, competitive. No one dominated. Gate held as expected.

Inverted <45% + SPI broke — Looked balanced (Colombia 38%) but underdogs flipped the result. Hyper game readiness.
Step 3: Test Carryover (TCI)
Does group environment predict knockout success?
Finally we test whether teams from certain GEI types perform differently in knockouts. Did coming from a Suppressed group help or hurt? Did Dominant group winners keep winning?
