📐 The TCI Methodology — NTG Framework

ⓘ What is TCI?

The Tournament Carryover Index (TCI) is a predictive metric developed by Name The Game (NTG) under the Natural Technical Governance (NTG) framework. TCI asks: Does a team's group-stage environment predict their knockout success?

TCI is built on two verified indices — SPI and GEI — combined into a single carryover score for each team.

📈 SPI — Strategic Performance Index

SPI measures whether the competitive ordering established by Matchday 2 was preserved in Matchday 3. If favorites held their positions, SPI is high and the group is "honest."

SPI = (% of seed positions held from Matchday 2 → 3)
✓ 83.3% held in WC 2026 groups (20 of 24 positions) — only Groups D and K inverted
Threshold: >66% = "held", ≤66% = "broke"

👥 GEI — Group Environment Index

GEI classifies each group by measuring how much the top team dominated goal-scoring. A high share means the group was flat or suppressed; a low share means it was open and competitive.

GEI = (Top Team Goals / Total Group Goals) × 100

Dominant >60% share — one team overwhelmed the group
Constrained 45–60% share — top team won a flat group
Open <45% share + SPI held — competitive, honest group
Inverted <45% share + SPI broke — competitive but upsets occurred

⚙ TCI Calculation

TCI = α(SPIteam) + β(GEIgroup) + γ(Knockout Path)

Where:
• SPIteam = did this team hold its Matchday 2 seed? (1/0)
• GEIgroup = group environment score (0–100 normalized)
• Knockout Path = progression depth bonus (0, 1, 2, 3, 4)
• α, β, γ = NTG-calibrated weights

💡 Current Observation

Teams emerging from Open + Honest groups (GEI <45%, SPI held) have shown stronger knockout performance in matches analyzed so far.
The Tournament Carryover Index continues to evaluate whether this pattern persists as additional knockout rounds are completed. Teams from dominant groups may be under-prepared for the intensity shift.
